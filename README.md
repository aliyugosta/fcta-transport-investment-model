# 🚌 FCTA Transport Sector Investment Model

> **Business Question:** Is a large-scale multi-modal transport infrastructure investment in Abuja financially viable — and what does the 10-year return look like for international investors?

---

## 🗂 Project Overview

Built a **comprehensive 10-year financial model** for a proposed multi-modal transport infrastructure investment in Abuja, Nigeria, commissioned under the Federal Capital Territory Administration (FCTA) and JBN framework. The model was designed to support investor due diligence and capital raising decisions, with outputs in both NGN and USD to serve both local and international audiences.

## 🏗 Model Architecture

The model is structured across the following components:

### Revenue Projections
- Passenger volume forecasts by transport mode (taxi, bus, e-bike)
- Fare revenue modelling with phased growth assumptions
- Scenario-based exchange rate projections (10% annual NGN depreciation vs USD)

### Cost Structure
- **CAPEX:** Infrastructure, fleet, technology setup costs
- **OPEX:** Fuel, maintenance, staffing, insurance, regulatory costs
- Phased cost deployment aligned with rollout timeline

### Financial Statements
- Projected Income Statement (Year 1–10)
- Gross Profit and Net Earnings analysis
- Cash flow timeline with debt repayment schedule

### Valuation
- **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)** analysis using appropriate discount rate
- **Net Present Value (NPV)** per investment phase
- Sensitivity analysis on key variables (ridership, exchange rate, fuel cost)

## 💡 Business Recommendations

1. **Investment is viable long-term but front-loaded with risk** — Year 5 shows a dip in net earnings due to peak debt repayment; investors should be positioned for a 7–10 year horizon, not short-term returns
2. **Electric bikes offer the highest margin per unit** — lower OPEX relative to buses makes e-bike expansion the most capital-efficient growth lever in Years 3–6
3. **Currency hedging is critical** — the 10% annual NGN depreciation assumption significantly erodes USD returns; international investors should negotiate USD-denominated revenue tranches or hedging instruments
4. **Phase 1 ridership targets are the key risk** — NPV is highly sensitive to Year 1–2 passenger volumes; a conservative ridership ramp-up plan with government backing agreements would derisk the investment
